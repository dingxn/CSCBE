CS Credit By Exam

Eligibility Criteria and Application Process

1. General Guidelines

1.1 Eligibility Criteria
  • Prior Coursework Requirement: CBE consideration is given only when a student has previously taken a similar course, but the credits cannot be used for graduation at NJIT. Examples:
    • The course was not transferred to NJIT
    • Course credits expired
    • The student switched programs and took a similar course for their previous program
  • Prerequisites Requirement: Student must meet all prerequisites for a course to be eligible for its CBE.
  • Ineligibility Conditions:
    • Students who have previously enrolled in a course at NJIT (current or past semester), unless the credits have expired, are not eligible for the CBE for that course.
    • If a student fails the CBE, they may not retake it and must complete the course at NJIT.
1.2 Registration and Approval
  • How to Register: Register using NJIT's Credit-by-Exam Request Form.
  • Eligibility Explanation: After registering, forward the confirmation email to your advisor and the approver (see below). In your message, clearly explain your eligibility for the CBE based on the above criteria. This is required for approval.
  • Fee: A $50 exam fee will be charged upon request approval.
  • Cancellation and Refund: To cancel a CBE registration or request a fee refund (no-show), contact the Office of the Registrar.
  • Grading: The CBE is graded pass/fail. To pass:
    • "C" or better for most courses
    • "B" or better for CS100
  • Course Credits: Successfully completed exams are recorded as transfer credit ("T") with no letter grade.

If you have questions, contact CS CBE Approver: Xiaoning Ding, Associate Professor, at xiaoning.ding@njit.edu, or your YWCC advisor at ywcc-advising@njit.edu

2. Course-Specific Guidelines

2.1 CS 100 Roadmap to Computing

Students must demonstrate proficiency in the material covered in CS 100 Roadmap to Computing by either:

  • Taking CS 100 at NJIT or
  • Taking the CS 100 Credit By Exam and scoring 80% or better

Preparation materials:

  1. Brief Course Description
  2. Practice exams: Practice Exam 1, Practice Exam 2, Practice Exam 3
  3. Additional: Lecture notes and Sample homework

Recommended steps:

  1. Go through practice exams and answer all problems.
  2. Check answers using an interpreter.
  3. If your answers differ, investigate your code tracing.

Additional resources: On-campus tutoring, Codecademy

The exam is offered at the end of Fall/Spring/Summer semesters. Registration closes one week before the exam date.
